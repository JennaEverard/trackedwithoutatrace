Browser Cookies

Small data files that store information about us on the internet

Supercookies

Cookies added to an HTTP request by an Internet Service Provider after it leaves the client's machine. Also known as Unique Identifier Headers (UIDHs).

Browser Fingerprints

A collection of device characteristics (i.e. Screen Size, Operating System, etc.) that can be compiled to uniquely identify an individual's device

ABOUT THE PROJECT

The internet was intended to be a place...

where knowledge could be shared and consumed freely. However, in our modern, highly networked world, the internet seems to have become almost synonymous with a lack of privacy and, as such, a lack of this freedom. This invasion of privacy has come in the forms of various tracking technologies, namely cookies, super cookies, and browser fingerprinting. When these technologies were first introduced, widespread tracking mechanisms seemed like a thing of science fiction, but as we now know, time would prove this notion wrong.

This study aims to further investigate the privacy implications of cookies and browser fingerprinting and gain insights into the cognizance and perception of the average internet user. By carrying out a series of surveys, interviews, and fingerprinting simulations, we hope to initiate a collaborative thought process towards strategies, measures, and regulations that both individuals and larger communities can adapt to promote online privacy.

How to be a Tougher Cookie Online

TURN OFF / DELETE COOKIES

Clearing third party cookies will limit tracking and is a good privacy practice!

USE PRIVATE BROWSING

By default, private browsing / incognito mode will delete all activity and temporary files

TRY A VPN

A virtual private network (VPN) will mask your IP address

DISABLE JAVASCRIPT

This will prevent websites from detecting certain characteristics, such as what plugins and fonts are installed.

REVIEW EXTENSIONS

Having more extensions is likely to make you more unique, so opt for less in order to make it harder for browsers to fingerprint you.

OPT FOR FARBLING

Some browsers (i.e. Brave) introduce randomness to signal outputs to constantly change a user's fingerprint - a technique called farbling.
